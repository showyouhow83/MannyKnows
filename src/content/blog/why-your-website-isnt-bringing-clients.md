If your website isn't bringing you clients, it isn't a brochure — it's a bill you pay for nothing. For contractors, clinics, law firms, and daycares, the reasons a site underperforms are almost always the same five. Here they are, worst first, with the fix for each.

## 1. It's too slow — especially on a phone

Most people find you on their phone, and they will not wait. A few extra seconds of load time and they're gone, back to Google, on to the next result. Slow sites also **rank** worse, so speed costs you twice: fewer visitors, and fewer of them staying. **The fix:** a site built for near-perfect speed scores — lightweight, optimized images, no bloated page builders — and kept that way, not just fast the week it launched.

## 2. Google can't tell what you do or where you do it

If your site is one vague "Services" page with no clear location, Google has nothing specific to rank. Someone searching "estate lawyer Springfield" or "roof repair in Chicopee" never sees you, because nothing on your site matches. **The fix:** a real page for each service, written around what people actually search, with your service area made obvious — plus the technical SEO (structure, metadata, local signals) that tells Google exactly who to show you to.

This now decides more than your Google rank. When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overview for "an estate lawyer in Springfield," the assistant can only put forward a business whose site plainly says what it does and where. That's answer-engine optimization, and it runs on the very same clear structure and schema — a vague site is invisible to the AI for the same reason it's invisible to Google. Fix it once and you become findable in both.

## 3. A visitor can't quickly tell they should trust you

People decide in seconds. If your site looks dated, has no real photos, no reviews, and — for a doctor or lawyer — no credentials or real bios, they bounce, no matter how good you actually are. Trust is the whole job of a service-business site. **The fix:** real photos of your work or team, your best Google reviews front and center, clear credentials, and a clean, current design that signals you're a real, active business.

## 4. There's no obvious next step

Even interested visitors leave if you make them hunt for how to reach you. A phone number buried in the footer, no form, no "book" button — that's a lead lost to friction. **The fix:** an obvious call-to-action on every page — tap-to-call, a short form, a booking button — so the next step is always one tap away.

## 5. Nothing answers after you've closed

Your best leads often show up at night and on weekends. If there's nothing there to answer them, they go cold before you open. **The fix:** an **AI Sales & Booking Agent** that answers questions and books the appointment 24/7 — so the visitor who found you at 10 p.m. is a booked job by morning, not a missed one.

## The pattern underneath all five

Notice what these have in common: none of them are about how the site *looks*. They're about whether it's **fast, found, trusted, and easy to act on** — and whether it keeps working after launch. A pretty site that fails all five still brings in nothing. That's why we treat a website as something you maintain, not something you buy once and forget.
