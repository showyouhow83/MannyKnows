Automation gets oversold. You don't need an AI for everything — you need to stop doing the handful of repetitive tasks that quietly burn hours every week. Here are the five we start with.

## 1. Lead intake

Every new inquiry should be logged, tagged, and assigned without anyone retyping it. If a human is retyping details from an email into a spreadsheet, that's a bug.

## 2. Follow-ups

The money is in the follow-up, and the follow-up is what everyone forgets. Automated reminders and sequences mean no warm lead goes cold because it was a busy Tuesday.

## 3. Scheduling

Back-and-forth to book a time is a tax on both sides. A self-serve scheduler that respects your real availability removes it entirely.

## 4. Invoicing and reminders

Send, track, and nudge on unpaid invoices automatically. Getting paid shouldn't require you to remember who owes you.

## 5. Reporting

Instead of building the same report by hand each month, have it assembled and waiting. Decisions get faster when the numbers are already on the table.

## The rule of thumb

If you do it the same way more than a few times a week, it's a candidate. Start with one, get it boringly reliable, then move to the next.
